# How to Prompt AI to Write Your Company Mission Statement

Source: https://copyhackers.com/ai-prompt/prompt-ai-to-write-a-company-mission-statement/
Author: Kay del Rosario
Reviewer: Chris Silvestri

---

## Overview

This tutorial teaches you to use GPT-4 to generate customer-facing mission statements in approximately 10 minutes. The process produces multiple versions tailored for different marketing channels.

---

## Step 1: Answer Questions About Your Company

Complete the following template in a word processor. Do not include brackets or instructional text when using Step 2's prompt.

```
Our company name is: [Your company name]

Our primary customers are: [Who are they? Example: young parents,
college students, or small business owners?]

Our primary customers' key needs and values are: [What matters to them?
Example: affordability, quality, convenience, or sustainability?]

The unique benefits we offer to our customers include: [What sets you apart?
Example: 24/7 support, innovative solutions, or cost-effective products?]

We stand out from our competitors by: [How do you differentiate?
Example: superior quality, lower prices, or unique experience?]

The core values driving our business include: [What guides you?
Example: integrity, innovation, customer satisfaction, or community engagement?]

Our core values benefit our customers: [How do these values help them?
Example: sustainability commitment leads to safer, higher-quality products?]
```

---

## Step 2: Prompt AI to Create Your Mission Statement

### Full Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready)

```
You are a conversion copywriter with marketing and branding expertise.
You understand brand identity, messaging, values, personality, and
target audiences. You excel at creating persuasive copy that builds
positive brand perception and encourages long-term loyalty.

You're a brand voice expert capable of adjusting responses to match
desired brand voices while maintaining consistency across platforms.

Your task: Take the information provided and write multiple versions
of a customer-facing mission statement.

Write versions as follows:

1. Core Message: Identify the core message encapsulating your value
proposition. Ensure consistency across all versions.

2. Long Version (Website, Brochures): Provide detailed explanation
of mission, values, and customer benefits. Tell a story or provide
examples illustrating your mission in action.

3. Medium Version (Social Media Bio, Email Signatures): Write a few
sentences conveying mission essence and values. Include a call to action
or engagement question.

4. Short Version (Social Media Posts, Headings): Create a slogan or
tagline encapsulating your mission. Make it memorable, catchy, and
instantly understandable.

5. Micro Version (Logos, Business Cards): Choose a keyword or short
phrase representing your mission. Incorporate visuals reinforcing the
message if possible.

Write the customer-facing mission statements with these characteristics:

Tone: [e.g. energetic, friendly, serious]
Voice: [e.g. aristocratic, leader, teacher]
Reading level: [e.g. fifth grade, eighth grade]

Write the customer-facing mission statement based on this information:

[Paste completed Step 1 work here]
```

---

## Step 3: Deploy Your Mission Statement

Use the generated content as-is or edit for closer brand alignment. Apply versions across:

- Website and brochures (long version)
- Social media bios and email signatures (medium version)
- Social posts and headings (short version)
- Logos and business cards (micro version)
